What are we going to study?

In this interactive activity you will review the first civilizations of the Ancient Age: Mesopotamia and Egypt. You will practise important facts about rivers, writing, society, religion, art, pyramids, sculpture and life after death.

You will also learn and revise English vocabulary with definitions in English.

VOCABULARY IN ENGLISH

Vocabulary

All terms and definitions are in English.

Hydraulic civilizations
Civilizations that grew and prospered thanks to the use of river water.
Ancient Age
A historical period that began when writing was invented.
Cuneiform writing
An early writing system from Mesopotamia made with wedge-shaped signs.
Hieroglyphic writing
A writing system from Ancient Egypt that used pictures and symbols.
Scribe
A person who wrote documents and kept records in Ancient Egypt.
City-state
An independent city and the land around it with its own government.
Ziggurat
A stepped temple built in Mesopotamia.
Upper Egypt
The southern part of Ancient Egypt.
Lower Egypt
The northern part of Ancient Egypt, near the Nile delta.
Pharaoh
The ruler of Ancient Egypt.
Polytheism
The belief in more than one god.
Ka
The soul in Ancient Egyptian beliefs.
Mummification
The process of preserving a dead body.
Mastaba
An early Egyptian tomb with a simple rectangular shape.
Pyramid
A monumental tomb with triangular sides built for Egyptian pharaohs.
Hypogeum
An underground tomb with chambers and corridors.
Afterlife
Life after death.
Sarcophagus
A stone coffin where a mummy was placed.
Canopic jars
Special jars used to keep organs during mummification.
Temple
A building dedicated to the gods.
Sculpture
Art made by carving or shaping materials such as stone or wood.
Relief
A type of sculpture carved on a flat surface.
Mummy
A preserved dead body in Ancient Egypt.
Funerary goods
Objects buried with the dead for use in the afterlife.
Nile
The river that was essential for life and agriculture in Ancient Egypt.
Delta
A flat area where a river splits into branches before reaching the sea.
Flood
The overflowing of river water over land.
Harvest
The time when crops are collected from the fields.
